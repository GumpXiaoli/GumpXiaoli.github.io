Sub-grid Scale Models

Functional (eddy–viscosity) models​

The deviatoric part of τij\tau_{ij}τij​ is modeled as:

τij−13τkkδij=−2νtSij\tau_{ij} - \frac{1}{3}\tau_{kk}\delta_{ij} =-2\nu_{t} S_{ij}τij​−31​τkk​δij​=−2νt​Sij​

where νt\nu_{t}νt​ is the turbulent eddy viscosity and Sij=12(∂ui∂xj+∂uj∂xi)S_{ij} = \frac{1}{2}(\frac{\partial u_{i}}{\partial x_{j}} + \frac{\partial u_{j}}{\partial x_{i}})Sij​=21​(∂xj​∂ui​​+∂xi​∂uj​​) is the rate of strain tensor.

Smagorinsky-Lilly model​

The first SGS model developed was the Smagorinsky-Lilly SGS model, which was developed by Smagorinsky and used in the first LES simulation by Deardorff. It models the eddy viscosity as:

νt=(Cs△g)22SijSij=(Cs△g)2∣Sˉ∣\nu _{t} = (C_{s}\bigtriangleup_{g})^{2}\sqrt{2S_{ij}S_{ij}}=(C_{s}\bigtriangleup_{g})^{2}\left | \bar{S} \right |νt​=(Cs​△g​)22Sij​Sij​​=(Cs​△g​)2​Sˉ​

where △g\bigtriangleup_{g}△g​ is the grid size and CsC_{s}Cs​ is Smagorinsky consant (usually varies between 0.1 and 0.2).

LBM Implementation​

The total viscosity is:

νtotal=ν0+νt=ν0+(Cs△g)2∣Sˉ∣\nu_{total} = \nu_{0} + \nu_{t}=\nu_{0}+(C_{s}\bigtriangleup_{g})^{2}\left | \bar{S} \right |νtotal​=ν0​+νt​=ν0​+(Cs​△g​)2​Sˉ​

τtotal=τ0+3(Cs△g)2∣Sˉ∣\tau_{total}=\tau_{0} + 3(C_{s}\bigtriangleup_{g})^{2}\left | \bar{S} \right |τtotal​=τ0​+3(Cs​△g​)2​Sˉ​
