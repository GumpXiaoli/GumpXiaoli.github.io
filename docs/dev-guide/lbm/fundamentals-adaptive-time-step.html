Fundamentals of Adaptive Time Step in LBM

In LBM, the kinematic shear viscosity given by the relaxation time τ\tauτ as:

ν=cs2(τ−△t2)\nu = c_{s}^{2}(\tau - \frac{\bigtriangleup t}{2})ν=cs2​(τ−2△t​)

If assuming △t∗=1\bigtriangleup t^{*} = 1△t∗=1 and △x∗=1\bigtriangleup x^{*} = 1△x∗=1 in lattice unit, then we have:

ν=cs∗2(τ∗−12)(△x)2△t=ν∗(△x)2△t\nu = c_{s}^{*2}(\tau^{*} - \frac{1}{2})\frac{(\bigtriangleup x)^{2}}{\bigtriangleup t} = \nu^{*} \frac{(\bigtriangleup x)^{2}}{\bigtriangleup t}ν=cs∗2​(τ∗−21​)△t(△x)2​=ν∗△t(△x)2​

BGK Case​

The viscous stress tensor can be calculated from fif_{i}fi​ as:

σαβ=−(1−△t2τ)Παβ(1)≈−(1−△t2τ)∑iciαciβfineq\sigma_{\alpha \beta} = -(1 - \frac{\bigtriangleup t}{2\tau}) \Pi_{\alpha \beta}^{(1)} \approx -(1 - \frac{\bigtriangleup t}{2\tau})\sum_{i}c_{i\alpha}c_{i\beta}f_{i}^{neq}σαβ​=−(1−2τ△t​)Παβ(1)​≈−(1−2τ△t​)i∑​ciα​ciβ​fineq​

During adaptive time step, the real viscous stress tensor should be kept same:

σαβ=Cσ,oσαβ,o∗=Cσ,nσαβ,n∗\sigma_{\alpha \beta}=C_{\sigma,o}\sigma_{\alpha \beta,o}^{*}=C_{\sigma,n}\sigma_{\alpha \beta,n}^{*}σαβ​=Cσ,o​σαβ,o∗​=Cσ,n​σαβ,n∗​

If we have:

Generally, we have:

Cσ=ρ0(△x)2(△t)2,Cν=(△x)2△tC_{\sigma} = \rho_{0}\frac{(\bigtriangleup x)^{2}}{(\bigtriangleup t)^{2}}, C_{\nu}=\frac{(\bigtriangleup x)^{2}}{\bigtriangleup t}Cσ​=ρ0​(△t)2(△x)2​,Cν​=△t(△x)2​

fi,nneq,∗fi,oneq,∗=τn∗τo∗△tn△to\frac{f_{i,n}^{neq,*}}{f_{i,o}^{neq,*}} =\frac{\tau_{n}^{*}}{\tau_{o}^{*}}\frac{\bigtriangleup t_{n}}{\bigtriangleup t_{o}}fi,oneq,∗​fi,nneq,∗​​=τo∗​τn∗​​△to​△tn​​
